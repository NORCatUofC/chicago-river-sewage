Is there raw sewage being dumped in the Chicago River right now?

What is this?

Every so often when Chicago gets a lot of rain or there's a significant snowmelt, the Chicagoland water management agencies pump excess wastewater into the lake and river in order to prevent flooding.


The Chicago River | Source: Chicago Magazine

It's because of practices like these that the city reversed Chicago River's flow in 1848 to avoid contaminating our drinking supply. As a result, a significant amount of 'nutrient pollution' from Chicago travels down the Illinois and Mississippi rivers and collects in the Gulf of Mexico in a dead zone roughly the size of New Hampshire.


The Chicago Tunnel and Resevoir | Source: Chicago Tribune

In 1975, the Metropolitan Water Reclamation District of Greater Chicago started building a massive Tunnel and Reservoir (dubbed TARP) to prevent this flooding, but is not scheduled to complete it until 2029. The EPA has since sued to push this deadline forward, but as of Jan 9, 2014 a judge ruled that the 2029 deadline will stand.


The TARP Plan | Source: Metropolitan Water Reclamation District

So for now, the Chicagoland water management agencies will keep dumping raw sewage in to our river and we'll keep notifying citizens when it happens.
